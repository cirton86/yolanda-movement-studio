Program Details

Explore each program in depth to find the right fit for your goals and current situation.

Who This Program Is For

This program is designed for adults who want to feel strong, capable, and confident in their bodies without the pressure of aesthetics-focused training. You might be:

  • Adults 40+ who want functional strength for daily life
  • People managing chronic pain (low back, knees, shoulders)
  • Those who feel intimidated by traditional gym environments
  • Anyone prioritizing long-term health over short-term intensity
  • Individuals who've been told they're "too old" to start strength training

Common Starting Points

"I have a stiff low back and want to move without fear"

"I want to stay active as I age, not slow down"

Tailored program based on your goals, schedule, and abilities. You'll know what we're working on, why it matters, and how it connects to your bigger picture.

What We Do Together

1
Assessment Phase (Weeks 1-2)

Movement screening, identify limitations, establish baseline strength, discuss pain patterns and goals

2
Foundation Building (Weeks 3-8)

Master fundamental movement patterns, build body awareness, develop technique-first habits, address mobility restrictions

3
Progressive Strength (Ongoing)

Gradually increase load and complexity, maintain technique focus, continue education on movement principles

Expected Milestones

  • 30 Days: Improved body awareness, reduced fear of movement, better understanding of pain signals
  • 90 Days: Noticeable strength gains, reduced chronic pain, improved daily function
  • 6+ Months: Sustained strength, confidence in movement, ability to self-manage minor setbacks

Common Questions

How often do I need to train?

Most clients see best results with 2 sessions per week. 1 session/week works for maintenance.

Will this help with my chronic pain?

Many clients experience reduced pain through improved movement patterns and strength. We work with your pain, not against it.

Who This Program Is For

This program bridges the gap between physical therapy discharge and confident, independent movement. You might be:

  • Recently discharged from PT but not feeling fully confident
  • Cleared to exercise but unsure how to progress safely
  • Afraid of reinjury or setbacks
  • Need continued guidance beyond PT's scope
  • Want to return to activities you did before injury

Common Starting Points

"PT helped, but I'm still nervous about reinjury"

"I don't know how to progress from PT exercises"

"I want to get back to hiking/sports/activities"

What We Do Together

1
PT Review & Assessment (Week 1)

Assess your movement patterns, discuss your history, and identify where you are versus where you want to be. Understanding your body's reality, not comparing to others.

2
Careful Progression (Weeks 2-12)

Continue PT exercises with gradual load increases, add functional movements, build confidence through small wins

3
Return to Activity (Ongoing)

Gradually reintroduce desired activities, learn to manage minor flare-ups, develop long-term maintenance strategies

Expected Milestones

  • 30 Days: Reduced fear of reinjury, successful progression beyond PT exercises
  • 90 Days: Return to modified versions of desired activities, improved confidence
  • 6+ Months: Full return to activities, ability to self-manage minor setbacks

Common Questions

Do I need to bring my PT discharge notes?

Helpful but not required. We'll assess where you are now and build from there.

What if I have a setback or flare-up?

We adjust immediately. Setbacks are part of the process, and we'll use them as learning opportunities.

Who This Program Is For

This program is for athletes and active individuals training for specific events who want to perform well while protecting their bodies. You might be:

  • Training for HYROX, marathons, or obstacle course races
  • Climbers preparing for competitions or big projects
  • Endurance athletes wanting injury prevention
  • Anyone with a specific event goal requiring strength support
  • Athletes who've been injured before and want to train smarter

Common Questions

How does this fit with my existing training?

We complement your event training, not replace it. Strength work is scheduled around your key workouts.

When should I start before my event?

Ideally 12-16 weeks out. We can work with shorter timelines, but more time allows better preparation.
