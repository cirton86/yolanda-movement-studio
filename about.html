About Yolanda

Certified Personal Trainer · Yoga & Pilates Instructor

My Mission

"My mission is to empower individuals to move with confidence, strength, and freedom. Through personalized training and holistic wellness, I help clients overcome pain, build resilience, and achieve their fitness goals in a supportive, inclusive environment."

Everyone is welcome here. Whether you're in your 20s or your 80s, recovering from injury or training for a marathon, just starting out or returning after years away — I meet you exactly where you are, not where you think you should be.

I help people build sustainable fitness through patient, educated training. My approach is rooted in the belief that small changes lead to positive, progressive direction—and that movement should support the life you want to live.

Holistic wellness integration. True fitness isn't just about exercise. My approach combines personal training, yoga, and Pilates to address movement, recovery, stress management, and lifestyle sustainability. I help you build a practice that supports your whole life — not just your workouts.

Whether you're navigating recovery, building strength for the first time, or training for a specific event, my focus is always the same: foundation, technique, and body awareness.

Fitness isn't about punishment or transformation. It's about understanding your own body, making small adjustments, and building confidence through discipline, patience, and education.

My Coaching Philosophy

Foundation and technique are key to support and promote growth and progress. Before we add weight or increase volume, we master the movement. This isn't about being perfect—it's about understanding what your body is doing and why it matters.

I give you information, and you give me feedback. This way you're learning your body. You should understand what you're doing and why. I help you recognize your body's signals and teach you how to progress safely.

Small changes lead to positive, progressive direction. Progress isn't linear, and that's normal. Sustainable fitness is built through consistency and patience, not urgency or intensity for its own sake.

Your body needs a break sometimes. Moderation and recovery are key. I teach my clients that just because it doesn't happen to the person you see at the gym hitting it hard doesn't mean it won't happen to you—so manage your intensity.

My Story: Learning Through Experience

In my mid-40s, I had a foot injury. Before this, I had very little pain and was intense with my workouts—I found enjoyment in them. The foot injury taught me that I need to slow down and that the body needs a break sometimes.

Not only did I learn a lot about foot health, I also gained a lot of compassion for the rehab stage and how prevention and rehab work together. Moderation and recovery are key.

This experience shaped how I coach. I emphasize that we may not be able to prevent every injury, but we can do our best incorporating exercises to promote a healthy body. I teach my clients that just because it doesn't happen to the person you see at the gym hitting it hard doesn't mean it won't happen—so manage your intensity.

I know what it's like to be uncertain, cautious, or frustrated with your body. That's why I prioritize safety, education, and building confidence one step at a time.

Why I Understand Recovery

My own recovery journey deepened my understanding of what it feels like to be uncertain, cautious, or frustrated with your body. That experience informs how I coach—with empathy, patience, and a realistic view of what progress actually looks like.

I know what it's like to be afraid of re-injury, to second-guess every movement, and to feel like you're starting over. That's why I prioritize safety, education, and building confidence one step at a time.
