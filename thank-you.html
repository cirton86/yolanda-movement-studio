A Letter of Gratitude

To the clients who made this journey possible

Dear friends,

When I first stepped into this work over twelve years ago, I had no idea how profoundly you would shape me.

I came to you with knowledge—certifications, techniques, an understanding of biomechanics. But you gave me something far greater. You gave me trust. You gave me your stories. You gave me the privilege of witnessing your courage.

Every time you showed up when your body hurt, when you were scared, when you didn't believe you could—you taught me what true strength looks like. It isn't found in a perfect squat or a flawless movement. It's in the decision to try again. To show up for yourself, even when no one else is watching.

You've taught me that healing isn't linear. That progress isn't always visible. That some days, simply staying present in your body is an act of defiance.

I've watched you reclaim movements you thought were lost forever. I've seen you lift weights that once felt impossible. I've witnessed the moment your face changes when you realize: I'm stronger than I thought.

Those moments? They're why I do this.

But more than that—you've believed in me when I was still learning. You trusted me with your pain, your frustration, your hope. You let me guide you, even when the path wasn't clear. And in doing so, you helped me become the trainer I am today.

Thank you for your patience. For your honesty. For showing up, session after session, year after year.

Thank you for trusting the process when it felt slow. For celebrating the small wins with me. For understanding that this work isn't about perfection—it's about progress, one movement at a time.

Thank you for letting me be part of your journey.

I don't take it lightly that you chose to spend your time, your energy, and your trust with me. In a world that constantly tells us we're not enough, you chose to invest in yourself. That takes courage. And I'm honored to walk alongside you.

Here's what I know: Your body is capable of more than you realize. Your strength—both physical and mental—is still growing. And the work we do together? It's just the beginning.

I can't wait to see where you go next.

With deep gratitude and unwavering belief in you,

Yolanda Reinert

NASM-CPT · Movement & Longevity Specialist
