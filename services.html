Personal Training & Longevity

Strength training for adults who want to feel capable, confident, and strong in their bodies—without the hype.

  • Adults of all ages (20s to 80s) who want to feel strong and capable
  • All fitness levels welcome — from complete beginners to active adults
  • People dealing with stiffness, aches, or uncertainty about exercise
  • Individuals who value guidance over intensity
  • Anyone intimidated by traditional gym environments
  • Those who want to build fitness as part of real life, not as a separate identity

All bodies welcome. This program focuses on function, strength, and how you feel — not aesthetics or weight loss.

We start with where you are—not where you think you should be. Every session prioritizes:

  • Technique and alignment before adding load
  • Movement quality over volume or intensity
  • Body awareness so you understand what you're feeling and why
  • Intelligent progression based on your readiness, not a fixed timeline
  • Education in every session—you should know what you're doing and why

After 30 days: Feel better and move better. Notice ease in moving around during daily activities.

After 90 days: Consistent reduced pain, feel stronger, see body changes, and improved energy levels.

Long-term vision: Ability to continue activities, try new activities, and don't feel age is a big hamper of life.

First Session Assessment

I watch you do 4 basic exercises (squat, lunge, push-up, plank) to see how you move, your alignment, and strength level. We discuss how you feel during the movement patterns, and I show you how to make it better.

Post-Physical Therapy Transition

Bridging the gap between physical therapy and real-world movement. For those who are medically cleared but not yet confident or fully functional.

Important: This is NOT physical therapy. This IS informed, careful, and intentional progression for those who have been discharged from PT and are ready for the next step.

What Inspired This Service

I felt there was a need for clients to understand and continue the exercises after PT sessions were complete. Clients told me I explained exercises well and helped them understand why to continue doing them. Some clients' physical therapists were happy with the work we had done together. Through these experiences, I felt I was able to connect PT to training in a way that really benefits clients.

  • Individuals who have been discharged from physical therapy
  • People who are medically cleared but not fully confident or functional
  • Those afraid of re-injury or doing exercises incorrectly
  • Anyone who needs continued guidance beyond PT but isn't ready for traditional training

Safe, supportive environment. This program prioritizes your recovery and confidence — we progress at your pace, not a predetermined timeline.

We continue and refine the work you started in physical therapy:

  • Continue PT exercises and progress them as you're ready
  • Teach you why exercises matter so you understand your body's needs
  • Pain-aware progression using a 1–10 scale
  • Earned progression, not rushed timelines

Specialties

  • Shoulder issues
  • Lower back issues
  • Upper thoracic alignment and activation/mobility
  • Hip mobility

After 4 weeks: Move better and are more aware of your body

After 8 weeks: Reduced pain and more range of motion

After 12 weeks: Further function in strength and range of motion

Full function: Pain is eliminated or reduced dramatically, able to go back to normal routine

Flare-ups are normal. They don't mean you're failing or going backward. We back it down, rest, and work through it after a few days.

You're not behind. Progress isn't linear, and your timeline is your own. We move forward when you're ready, not when a program says you should be.

Safety and confidence first. Every session reinforces that you're in control, you understand your body, and you're building trust in your movement again.

Performance & Event Training

Preparing for HYROX, obstacle courses, marathons, climbing, or other demanding events—built on a foundation of longevity and injury prevention.

  • HYROX athletes
  • Obstacle course racers
  • Runners (5K to marathon)
  • Climbers
  • Anyone training for a specific, demanding event

Performance with longevity. Train hard AND smart — this program builds strength and endurance without sacrificing your long-term health.

Performance training doesn't contradict injury-prevention values—it builds on them:

  • Event-specific strength and conditioning tailored to your goals
  • Mobility and joint preparation to support high-demand training
  • Structured progressions and deloads to prevent burnout and injury

I've personally completed 3 marathons, rock climbing (top rope, lead, bouldering), and obstacle course racing including Men's Health 10-mile OCR where I qualified for elite waves. I understand the physical and mental demands of training for performance.

I use 4-week training blocks with deloads after each block to prevent burnout and injury. This structured approach ensures you're building strength and endurance without sacrificing your long-term health.

You'll train hard, but you'll train smart. We'll build the strength, endurance, and resilience you need to perform—without sacrificing your long-term health.
